How to use the weekly tracker
A quick guide to getting the most out of your tracker.
The three columns

Each working week is organised into three columns. Add tasks to whichever column fits best — you can always move them later.

● In progressTasks you are actively working on right now. ● PlannedTasks you intend to start this week but haven't yet. ● BlockedTasks you cannot progress because something or someone is in the way. Use notes to explain the blocker.
Task buttons
✓Mark as completed — moves to the Completed section. ✗Mark as cancelled — for work dropped or outside your control. →Move to a different column — e.g. move a planned task to Blocked, or a blocked task back to In progress. ◇Mark as ongoing — automatically carries over to the next week. Turns solid (◆) when active. Use for long-running work spanning weeks or months. +▲Add or edit a note. Notes support bullet points and strikethrough formatting. ↩Restore a completed or cancelled task back to its original column. ✕Permanently delete a task. This cannot be undone.
Notes and formatting

Click +▲ on any task to open the note editor. The toolbar provides two formatting options:

• ListInserts a bullet point at the cursor. Each bulleted line renders as a list item when saved. SWraps selected text in ~~ for strikethrough. Carries through to the copied manager update. Click again to remove.

Press Cmd+Enter (Mac) or Ctrl+Enter (Windows) to save a note quickly.

Tags
carriedTask was carried over from a previous week.
ongoingCarries over automatically each week until completed or cancelled.
Navigating weeks

Use the arrows at the top to move between weeks. Each week is stored separately so you can look back at any time. A blue banner appears if you have incomplete tasks from a previous week, offering to carry them all over in one click.

Manager update

The update preview builds automatically. Click Copy update to copy formatted text to your clipboard, ready to paste into an email, Teams, or Slack. Strikethrough in notes is preserved using ~~ markers in the plain text copy.

Saving your data

Data saves automatically to your browser's local storage on every change. Use Export JSON to download a backup at any time, and Import JSON to restore it on any device.

Import JSON
Paste a previously exported JSON file to restore your data. This will overwrite your current data.
